ADEQ Toolbar — Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 7, 2026 · Last updated: May 7, 2026 · Contact: mgargiulo@adeqmedia.com

1. Introduction and scope

ADEQ Toolbar ("the extension", "we", "us") is a private Chrome extension developed by ADEQ Media for the exclusive use of authorized employees performing internal B2B prospecting and outreach. This Privacy Policy describes in detail what data the extension collects, how it is collected, used, stored, shared, retained and how authorized users can exercise their rights over that data.

The extension is not a public consumer product. Sign-in is restricted by an explicit email allowlist of ADEQ Media staff accounts. Anyone outside the allowlist who installs the extension cannot authenticate and the extension performs no data collection for them.

2. Information we collect

2.1. Information collected directly from the authorized employee

  • Authentication credentials: the work email address and a password (managed by Supabase Auth) used to sign into the extension. The password is hashed by Supabase and is never visible to the extension code.
  • OAuth access tokens: when the employee enables Gmail sending, Google issues an OAuth 2.0 access token granting permission to send messages on behalf of the signed-in employee. The token is stored in chrome.storage.local and refreshed automatically by Chrome's identity API.
  • User preferences and history: recently analyzed domains, custom keyword lists, draft pitches, UI settings — all stored in chrome.storage.local on the employee's own machine.
  • Telemetry of toolbar actions: timestamps and counts of API calls, emails sent, prospects pushed to CRM. This is used to enforce per-user quotas and to display usage dashboards to administrators.

2.2. Information collected from the websites the employee analyzes

  • The current tab's URL, title and meta description at the moment the employee chooses to analyze it. The extension does not run in the background on other tabs and does not collect history of pages the employee did not analyze.
  • Visible ad-tech signals (e.g. presence of Google AdSense / Google Ad Manager / Prebid scripts, banner sizes) read from the page DOM.
  • Public contact information scraped from the same domain's contact page (e.g. info@example.com) for outreach purposes.

2.3. Information retrieved from third-party APIs

The extension queries the following third-party APIs to enrich each analyzed domain:

3. How information is collected

  • The employee actively initiates every analysis by clicking the toolbar icon. The extension never analyses, scrapes or transmits data from a tab unless the employee opens the side panel for that tab.
  • The employee's email is collected when they sign in via the login form.
  • Third-party API responses are received over HTTPS only.
  • The extension does not deploy any tracking pixel, cookie, fingerprinting, beacon, behavioural analytics or session-replay technology.

4. How information is used

  • To authenticate the employee against ADEQ Media's allowlist.
  • To display enriched prospect data in the side panel (traffic, category, contact, draft pitch).
  • To send sales emails from the employee's authenticated Gmail account, on the employee's explicit click of the "Send via Gmail" button.
  • To create or update CRM records in ADEQ Media's private Monday.com board, on the employee's explicit click of the push button.
  • To enforce usage limits (per-user daily caps for emails sent and CRM pushes; team-wide monthly cap on third-party API requests) preventing abuse and overage.
  • To produce internal admin dashboards showing aggregated team activity (sites analyzed, emails sent, time spent in autopilot).

We do not use any collected data for advertising, profiling, retargeting, credit scoring, lending, employment decisions, insurance, or for any purpose unrelated to ADEQ Media's internal sales operation.

5. How information is stored

  • On the employee's device: session tokens, cached lookups, keyword lists and drafts are stored in chrome.storage.local. This data is removed automatically by Chrome when the extension is uninstalled.
  • On Supabase (Postgres + Auth): employee authentication data, encrypted at rest by Supabase, protected by Row-Level Security policies scoped to authenticated ADEQ Media accounts. Hosted in EU region.
  • On Supabase Edge Functions: third-party API keys are kept as server-side secrets and are never exposed to the extension client. The extension proxies all paid API calls through this function, so keys never reach the employee's browser.
  • On Monday.com: CRM records under ADEQ Media's account. Access controlled by Monday.com's permissions.

6. How information is shared

  • We do not sell, rent, lease, trade or otherwise transfer collected data to brokers, advertisers, or any third party for monetary or non-monetary consideration.
  • We share data only with the third-party providers listed in section 2.3 and only to the extent necessary to perform their function (e.g. sending the analyzed domain to SimilarWeb to retrieve traffic data). Each provider operates under its own privacy policy and security controls.
  • We do not transfer data to law enforcement except where required by valid legal process applicable to ADEQ Media.
  • The extension does not include any analytics SDKs (no Google Analytics, no Mixpanel, no Sentry, no Crashlytics).

7. Data retention and deletion

  • Local cache of analyzed-domain results expires automatically after 90 days, or earlier if the employee clears the extension's storage.
  • Session tokens are refreshed every hour and invalidated on sign-out.
  • Activity logs on Supabase (used for the admin dashboards and quota enforcement) are retained for the duration of the ADEQ Media account. An employee may request earlier deletion of their personal log entries by emailing the contact below.
  • OAuth tokens issued by Google are revoked when the employee signs out or removes the extension's access from their Google Account permissions page.

8. Your rights

Authorized ADEQ Media employees can, at any time:

  • Sign out and revoke the OAuth grant.
  • Clear all locally stored extension data via Chrome's extension settings.
  • Request a copy of, or deletion of, their personal records held in the ADEQ Media Supabase project, by writing to the contact below.
  • Object to specific processing by ceasing to use the extension.

9. Permissions justification (Chrome Web Store)

  • activeTab + scripting — read URL/title/meta only of the tab the employee actively opens the side panel on.
  • tabs — open shortlisted prospect URLs in background tabs when the employee triggers a bulk action.
  • storage — local cache for session, drafts, settings.
  • identity — Google OAuth so the employee can send sales emails from their own Gmail account.
  • sidePanel — render the toolbar UI as a side panel.
  • <all_urls> host permission — the team prospects across the open web; we cannot predict in advance which publisher domain the employee will analyze. Access is still strictly per-tab and only when the employee opens the side panel.

10. Security measures

  • All network traffic uses HTTPS / TLS 1.2+.
  • Third-party API keys live as server-side Supabase secrets — never bundled in the extension and never exposed to the browser.
  • Supabase Row-Level Security restricts every table to authenticated ADEQ Media accounts.
  • Gmail identity is checked against the signed-in Supabase email before any email is sent, preventing one employee from sending from another's mailbox.
  • The extension contains no remotely loaded code; the entire bundle is reviewed before each Chrome Web Store submission.

11. Children's privacy

ADEQ Toolbar is an internal corporate tool for adult employees of ADEQ Media. It is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect any data from anyone under the age of 16. If we discover that data from a minor has been collected in error, it will be deleted immediately upon notification.

12. International data transfers

ADEQ Media operates from Latin America. Supabase resources are hosted in the EU. Third-party providers (Google, Apollo, Anthropic, RapidAPI vendors) operate globally; data sent to them may be processed in the United States or other jurisdictions where they maintain infrastructure. By using the extension, employees consent to such cross-border processing for the limited purposes described above.

13. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the latest revision. Material changes will be communicated by email to all allowlisted ADEQ Media accounts. Continued use of the extension after a change constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
